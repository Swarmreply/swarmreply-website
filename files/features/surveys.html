Frequently asked questions

What is an NPS survey?

Net Promoter Score asks one simple question — how likely are you to recommend us — on a 0–10 scale. It's the fastest way to read customer sentiment and spot issues before they become public 1-star reviews.

How do surveys connect to getting reviews?

SwarmReply uses the survey as a smart gate: customers who are thrilled (promoters) are guided to leave a public review, while those who aren't get a private channel to tell you what went wrong — so you can fix it before it's posted publicly.

Is this 'review gating'? Is it allowed?

No. Review gating means only inviting happy customers to post — that's against Google's policy. SwarmReply invites everyone honestly; the survey simply routes feedback to the most useful place. Unhappy customers can still post publicly if they choose.

Where do customers take the survey?

Anywhere — a one-tap link in an email or text, or a hosted survey page. It's mobile-first and takes seconds, which is why response rates are far higher than a long questionnaire.

Can I see trends over time?

Yes. Your dashboard tracks NPS, response rate, and the breakdown of promoters, passives, and detractors, so you can see whether your customer experience is improving month over month.
